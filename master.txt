The Approach
My approach: heal the system, not the symptom.
Most coaches give you tactics. Most consultants give you a deck. I do both. We figure out what’s actually in the way (often it’s not what you think), then we build the strategy around the human leading it. You can build something great and lose yourself in the problem. And then what was the point?

1. Drop In: We start with breath, presence, and what’s alive for you today.
2. Go Deep: We work the real issue — brand, business, or self — with the right tool for the moment.
3. Take One Small Step: You leave with one action. Small steps lead to big change.

About Jennie
Storyteller, magic-maker, and the coach you call when you’re building something brave and don’t want to lose yourself doing it.
Bio
I started in big rooms. Five years running 50+ events a month across the Pepsi Center, Dick's Sporting Goods Park, and the Paramount Theater taught me how to hold a crowd and make a logistics machine feel like an experience. From there I moved to a marketing agency, learned to braid experiential, digital, and brand into one voice, and landed at Sola Salon Studios as their first in-house marketing hire.
I spent nearly a decade at Sola Salon Studios building the brand, leading the team, and eventually serving as CMO of the parent company. After that chapter, I built a small fractional CMO practice, took real time off when my daughter was born, and trained as a coach at the Institute for Coaching Mastery (certified 2024).
Today I run a small, intentional practice as a holistic brand and leadership coach. Part strategist, part guide, part ritual-keeper. Mom of two. Still learning.
Values
My five North Stars. They shape how I show up — with my clients, my kids, and myself.

Connection: The work doesn’t move until two humans actually drop in.
Joy: Joy is a strategy. It’s also why my LLC is named Joyous Wolf.
Wellbeing: You can’t lead what you can’t sustain. Boundaries first.
Authenticity: The version of you that’s already here is the one we’re building from.
Community: Success unshared is failure. Every tool I’ve learned belongs in someone else’s hands, too.
My Process
My process is intentionally part-coach, part-consultant, part-ritual-keeper. Most of my clients get all three in the same session.
1. Drop In
Every session starts with a moment of presence. Sometimes a breath, sometimes a guided meditation, sometimes just a deep exhale and a question. We don’t rush. My sessions are 75 minutes on purpose.
2. Diagnose the Whole System
Whatever you bring: a hiring decision, a launch, a stuck moment, a story you can’t shake — we look at it the way a holistic practitioner looks at a body. The symptom is real, but rarely the root. We find the root.
3. Coach + Consult
This is where my background as a CMO and my training as a coach work together. Sometimes the right tool is a somatic practice. Sometimes it’s a positioning framework. Sometimes it’s, “your pricing is wrong, here’s what I’d do.” I don’t pretend to only wear one hat.
4. One Small Step
Every session ends with a single action — sometimes as small as “start drinking water.” Small steps, repeated over a three-month container, are what create the shift.
5. Close With Ritual
Coaching containers end with an intentional, non-religious ritual ceremony — a way of marking what you built and what you’re leaving behind. It’s the part most clients tell me about months later.

FAQ
Are sessions virtual or in-person?
Both. Most 1:1 work happens over Zoom. Team work, rituals, and brand workshops are designed for in-person whenever possible.

Why are your sessions 75 minutes?
Because 60 feels rushed. Real shifts need a few minutes of “how are you” at the start and a few minutes to land at the end.

Are you a coach or a consultant?
Both, depending on what the moment needs. I’m ICM-certified as a coach and spent two decades as a brand and marketing leader. Most clients get both inside the same session.

Do you take on every client?
No. I work with a small number of people at a time so I can stay in the work with each of them. The clarity call is how we both decide if it’s a yes.

What does “ritual” actually mean here?
Intentional, non-religious practices. Breath, journaling, ceremony, gathering. Designed to mark a moment so the work actually integrates.

Where are you based?
Denver, Colorado. I work with clients across the U.S. and travel for select team and brand engagements.
